DATABASE MANAGEMENT WITH MS ACCESS

    INTRODUCTION

  • Computer systems replace outdated forms of paper communication and paper file storage. They are used as a way to store and manage large amounts of information digitally.

  • Computer databases are used to store, manage and analyze large amounts of information quickly and efficiently.

    •

    UNDERSTANDING DATABASE

  • Whenever we make the list of information, such as names, addresses, products or invoices, we are in fact creating a database.

  • Records kept in a computer do not necessarily make a database.

  • A database is much more powerful than a simple list you make on paper or in a MS Word Document.

    •

    WHAT IS DATABASE?

  • A database is a collection of data that is organized in a manner that can be easily retrieved.

  • One basic feature of the database is that the data is organized in a systematic way such as a tabular format.

  • Database consists of fields(single piece of information), records(one complete set of fields) and files(collection of records), much like a telephone directory.
